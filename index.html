T Pal allows members to find a local friend in locations all around the world, or to be that local friend for other member travelers. For a daily fee plus expenses you can have a local resident sharing his knowledge of the place, helping you with planning and logistics when moving locally and providing support if there are linguistic and cultural barriers difficult to overcome.

Several different arrangements are possible, from a classic daytime cicerone, to a travel mate for local trips spanning several days, to a local friend, available via phone or email during office hours and for meetings at dinnertime or during the weekend. You can choose your local contact based on peer reviews of previous experiences and your direct knowledge of the T Pal member through his or her activity on the social platform and your direct interaction with him/her.

This initial round aims at raising the funds to design and implement the infrastructure and setup the operations centrally and in the regional locations where necessary. The infrastructure supports the operations on the web and on mobile devices, including texting on gsm networks, handling all interactions with travelers, guides and local coordinators. The operations require local coordinators and local partners for background checks, and advertising budget to reach both kind of customers (travelers and guides).

FAQ

How do you guarantee a safe experience with the hosts?
We guarantee the reliability of the guides through selection, background checks (within the limits of the local laws and regulations), and through an extremely transparent feedback system with customer reviews. The typical guide is a young professional or college student working with us part time.
What's a Local Coordinator?
A local coordinator is a T Pal member who coordinates and monitor the activity locally. Even though the operations relies entirely on the website and the organization is extremely flat and centralized, there is a need for a local presence to supervise the activities, finalize the selection process for the guides and intervene promptly on location when necessary.
